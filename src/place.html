    Axum Tsion

    The Axum Tsion church is situated close to the grounds of the
    Obelisks of Axum in the town of Axum, Tigray Region,
    northern Ethiopia. The first church is thought to have been
    erected in the fourth century AD during the reign of Ezana,
    the first Christian king of the Kingdom of Axum
    (current-day Eritrea and Ethiopia), and has subsequently been
    restored multiple times. As a result, women are not allowed into the
    "Old Church" due to the reservation that the Blessed Virgin Mary,
    who symbolizes the Ark, is the only woman permitted inside its grounds.



    Sof Omar

    The Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia's Oromia Region,
    in the Bale Zone of the Sof Omar Caverns, also known as Holqa Sof Omar,
    is home to the largest network of caves in all of Africa.
    One of the largest and most impressive subterranean caverns in the world,
    the Sof Omar Caves were created by the Weib River, which altered its
    course in the distant past and dug a new path through the limestone slopes.



    Title Name

    A group of people called the Hamar live in southwest Ethiopia.
    In the Debub Omo Zone of the Southern Nations, Nationalities,
    and Peoples Region, in the Hamer woreda (or district),
    a fertile area of the Omo River valley, they reside (SNNPR).
    Most of them are pastoralists. The Hamar are renowned for their distinctive
    "bull jumping" tradition, which marks a boy's transition into manhood.
    First, female relatives dance and invite newly initiated males to whip them;
    this demonstrates their support for the initiate and their scars give them
    the power to choose who they marry.

    Simien Mountain

    Northern Ethiopia is home to Simien Mountains National Escarpments, steep valleys,
    and a tall plateau make up its rough environment; these are the habitats of the
    uncommon Walia ibex and Gelada baboons. The majestic Ras Dashen mountain is
    among the tall peaks. Park trails lead to the camps at Sankaber, Geech, and Chenek.
    There are extensive views of the lowlands below and sheer cliffs from the
    central Gidir Got and Imet Gogo summit lookouts.

    Lalibela

    Lalibela is a town in Ethiopia's Amhara Region. It is a popular tourist destination
    because of its renowned rock-cut monolithic cathedrals and is situated in the Lasta region
    and North Wollo Zone. For Ethiopia's ancient, medieval, and post-medieval civilizations,
    Lalibela as a whole is a sizable and significant location. Lalibela is one of Ethiopia's
    holiest cities for Christians and a major pilgrimage destination.

    Axum Obelisk

    The Obelisk of Axum is a 4th-century CE, 24-metre (79 ft) tall phonolite[3] stele,
    weighing 160 tonnes (160 long tons; 180 short tons), in the city of Axum in Ethiopia.
